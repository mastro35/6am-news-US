### Oil Surges Past $100 as US-Iran Conflict Escalates After Red Sea Tanker Attacks

The U.S. war with Iran has intensified after Iran-backed Houthi militants in Yemen carried out drone attacks on two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea. In response to the escalating maritime threats, global oil prices surged, with Brent crude crossing the $100-per-barrel threshold for the first time in two months. President Donald Trump has warned of potentially massive retaliatory strikes, further stoking fears of broader energy disruptions and driving U.S. Treasury yields to their highest levels of his second term. The escalating tensions have disrupted global shipping routes, forcing tankers to bypass the Suez Canal—a detour costing millions of dollars and adding weeks to journeys.

### Trump Administration Unveils New Tariffs on Over 80 Countries

President Donald Trump has issued a new wave of tariffs ranging from 10% to 12.5% on more than 80 trading partners, including major allies like Canada, the UK, Australia, and the European Union. Framed as a crackdown on imports produced with forced labor, the new duties are designed to withstand legal challenges after the U.S. Supreme Court struck down a previous 10% blanket tariff in February. The move has sparked immediate pushback from international partners. Australia's trade minister called the regime unjustified, while Brazil and Canada labeled the tariffs arbitrary and are considering retaliatory measures.

### Central Park Stabbing Investigated as Possible Hate Crime

New York City police are investigating the daylight stabbings of two men on Manhattan’s Upper West Side as a possible hate crime. According to police, Morales yelled "Allahu Akbar" before stabbing the first victim with a knife and the second with a screwdriver. Both victims are expected to survive. Police Commissioner Jessica Tisch confirmed that the hate crimes unit is active in the investigation, highlighting heightened community anxieties amidst ongoing global tensions.

### Trump Ties Civilian Nuclear Deal to Saudi Arabia Joining Abraham Accords

A proposed landmark civilian nuclear cooperation agreement between the United States and Saudi Arabia has run into a major political hurdle. President Donald Trump announced a new condition requiring the kingdom to formally join the Abraham Accords and normalize diplomatic relations with Israel. The move has drawn criticism at home and in Israel, threatening to stall an agreement his administration announced just this week. The new demand puts Saudi Arabia in a geopolitical bind, as the kingdom has repeatedly stated that normalization is contingent on establishing a pathway to a Palestinian state—a prospect Israel's current government firmly rejects.

### UK Prime Minister Andy Burnham Launches Downing Street's Northern Branch

Newly poised British Prime Minister Andy Burnham has officially opened "No 10 North," a northern branch of Downing Street located in Manchester, marking a historic decentralization of British governance. On his first working day, Burnham chaired the inaugural meeting of a revived National Economic Council, designed to coordinate national growth and share decision-making power with regional mayors. The political shift has triggered a "Burnham bounce" in the UK, with GfK reporting the largest jump in consumer confidence in nearly three years as optimism spreads over the government's economic agenda.

### Wildfires Force Mass Evacuation of France’s Cap Ferret Peninsula

A highly unpredictable and intense wildfire has forced authorities in southwestern France to order the complete evacuation of the popular Cap Ferret peninsula. High winds have fanned the blaze, which has already consumed more than 10,000 hectares of land. Upward of 20,000 people, including thousands of summer tourists, have fled the Atlantic coast resort town by land and sea, utilizing hundreds of mobilized boats. Hundreds of firefighters, backed by military personnel, are battling the flames as dry heat continues to plague the region.
