### Google and Tesla Face Market Pressures Amid Heavy AI Spending

Tech giants are experiencing mixed financial fortunes as massive investments in artificial intelligence begin to weigh on profit margins. Google's parent company, Alphabet, reported a massive surge in quarterly revenue, driven by an 82% jump in its cloud division; however, its free cash flow turned negative due to spiraling capital expenditures on AI infrastructure, causing shares to slide. Similarly, Tesla reported falling quarterly profits despite a rebound in vehicle sales. The decline was attributed to deep vehicle price cuts and a substantial $5.8 billion spending spree on AI and robotics development, which pushed the automaker's cash flow into negative territory. Meanwhile, IBM lowered its annual revenue growth outlook as mainframe computer sales plunged 42%.
